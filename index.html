Biblical Teaching on Anger
Ephesians 4:26 — “Be angry and do not sin.”

Anger signals that something matters—often justice or boundaries. The command is not “never be angry” but don’t let anger master you. Move from reaction to response: pause, pray, plan.

  • Practice: 3 deep breaths → name the trigger → ask, “What would love do?”
  • Boundary tip: “I want to understand, but I won’t be yelled at. Let’s talk when we’re calmer.”
James 1:20 — “The anger of man does not produce the righteousness of God.”

Human anger alone rarely creates God’s good outcomes. We need God’s wisdom to transform anger into patient, truthful action.

  • Replace: reactivity → patience • accusation → questions • control → clarity.
Proverbs 15:1 — “A gentle answer turns away wrath.”

Tone matters. Gentleness is strength under control. Lower volume, slow pace, open posture.

Ecclesiastes 7:9 — “Be not quick in your spirit to become angry.”

Slowness creates space for the Spirit. Speed fuels sparks; slowness allows wisdom.

Righteous vs. Sinful Anger — Quick Checks
  • Righteous: aims for restoration, tells the truth, protects the weak, submits to Scripture.
  • Sinful: humiliates, controls, keeps score, refuses correction, seeks revenge.
Short Prayers
  • “Lord, slow my breath and speed my listening.”
  • “Father, set a guard over my mouth (Ps 141:3).”
  • “Spirit, show me a holy next step.”
Safety Note

If anger connects to abuse or addiction, seek trained help and set strong boundaries. Love does not enable harm.

Info — What this app does

Slow to Anger helps you track triggers, practice calm, learn Scripture’s way, and choose holy actions when mad.

  • Anger Tracker: journal triggers, intensity, responses; view weekly trends; export.
  • Calming: voice-guided prayers + box-breathing with visual timer.
  • Teaching: short, biblical notes (Eph 4:26; James 1:20).
  • Redeem your reactions: guided steps to plan a holy response and save it.

How to use

  1. Log today’s moments in Tracker. Be honest & brief.
  2. Do Calming (breath + prayer) before a hard convo.
  3. Read one Teaching card; practice the tip.
  4. Use Exercises to plan a response for recurring triggers.

Install on your phone

  • iPhone (Safari): Share → Add to Home Screen
  • Android (Chrome): Menu ⋮ → Add to Home Screen
